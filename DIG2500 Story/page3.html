You sign the waiver and make your way into the next room. 2 women sit at desks on opposite sides of the room. Both are sitting and typing out something on a keyboard with monitor screens you cannot see. In front of you is a desk with 2 buttons, one on the left side of the table and the other on the right, just like the woman.
The woman on the left notices your entrance immediately, looking up and waving. Her desk and outfit are immaculately pristine with not a single thing out of place, and her lightning blue eyes stare at you from behind her work before she continues typing away.
The woman on the right takes a minute to respond seemingly engulfed in her work. Her desk is less organized with sticky notes everywhere and a lipstick-stained coffee cup next to her. She waves at you and smiles with slightly smudged lipstick.
Over the intercom the robotic voice chimes in, 

       “Which is real: left or right? After you select your choice please continue to the next room.”
